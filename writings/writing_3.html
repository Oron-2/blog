Notes on building a forum with phpBB

25/12/21

I want to get familiarised with the process of using software, the primary purpose of which is to help make forums. Also, another main reason is that I like this type of "old" style forums more than social networks like Facebook, Twitter and so on, because, more or less, these sorts of platforms allow anyone to peek and enter your personal space. Also, in order to use them effectively, you need to add a large amount of private information. The good thing about the forums is that it is a place for communication between registered users without focusing on someone's personal information, but instead on the topic under discussion. Therefore, the driving force behind users who visit this type of forum is not about finding out about someone but about a genuine interest in a subject.

My search for software that helps create a forum showed me that most platforms work with PHP. In principle, I did not intend to embark on another technology because only JavaScript is difficult enough to keep me busy, but there seems to be no other choice. The good thing is that most things seem to be done through a UI panel, and I won't have to write PHP code. At least that's what it looks like at first glance.

The most popular, developed, and free platforms I have chosen are:

  • https://mybb.com/

    • “MyBB is the free and open source forum software powering thousands of engaging, vibrant, and unique communities across the internet.”

  • https://www.phpbb.com/

    • “phpBB is a free flat-forum bulletin board software solution that can be used to stay in touch with a group of people or can power your entire website. With an extensive database of user-created extensions and styles database containing hundreds of style and image packages to customise your board, you can create a very unique forum in minutes.”

  • https://flarum.org/

    • "Forums made simple. Modern, fast, and free!

  • https://www.simplemachines.org/

    • SMF is all that in one package, giving you an easy to use forum with many powerful features for you as well as your users. SMF is an open-source project made by volunteers from across the world.

After reading several articles and topics on the Internet about these technologies, I decided to focus on phpBB.

A few things to take in mind when developing the forum are:

  1. Installing a Bulgarian translation pack
  2. Choosing and integrating phpBB theme
  3. Checking helpful extensions
  4. Take a note on all categories that should be present
  5. Create a home page

Now the hard part is actually doing it.
